01 Business Problem

Public transport operators are subject to regulatory performance standards. Failure to meet timing-point targets, service reliability thresholds, or complaint response obligations can result in financial penalties, customer dissatisfaction, and reputational damage.

Operational performance information exists across multiple business functions. Route punctuality data is monitored through Mobile Statistics KPI reporting, ticketing device performance is reviewed through SAP and Qlik reporting, and customer complaints are supported by operational investigations using SAP service records and separate complaint-management workflows with strict SLA requirements.

Because these processes are reviewed independently, management lacks a consolidated view of operational performance, ticketing compliance issues, complaint trends, and emerging risks that could lead to regulatory penalties.

This project investigates how operational, customer, and compliance information can be integrated into a unified reporting solution that supports earlier intervention and better decision-making.

02 Stakeholders

Several stakeholder groups have a direct interest in how this problem is resolved:

Technology Development Team
Responsible for ticketing device monitoring, breach-code analysis, data quality reporting, and regulatory compliance support.
Customer Service Team Lead
Owns complaint logging; needs less manual re-keying and a categorisation scheme operations will actually accept.
Operations Administration
Responsible for KPI reporting, timing-point performance monitoring, SAP invoicing, receipting, cash and adjustment processing, and operational support.
Operations / Scheduling Manager
Responsible for punctuality KPI reporting through Mobile Statistics and requires complaint data that can be analysed at the same route and trip level.
Duty Managers
Act on service issues day-to-day; need to see complaint and KPI data together, not two weeks later in a meeting.
IT / Systems Support
Maintains operational data extracts and any new reporting layer; needs a solution that doesn't require changes to core operational systems.
Senior Management
Sponsors the monthly KPI report to the Board; needs a single trusted number and a defensible explanation of variance.

04 Pain Points

No shared key
Complaints are logged using free-text information, while operational systems use structured route identifiers, trip references, and service dates. The two cannot easily be joined without manual mapping.
Inconsistent categorisation
Complaint reason categories are chosen inconsistently between officers, making trend analysis unreliable.
Reporting lag
Reconciliation happens once a month, so a developing issue on a route isn't visible to Duty Managers until weeks later.
Manual effort
Two officers independently spend a full day each month producing summaries that still need to be reconciled by hand.
Disputed KPI
Unexplained variance between complaint volume and operational performance KPIs undermines confidence in the numbers reported to management.
No audit trail
Spreadsheet edits are untracked, so it's not possible to see who changed a categorisation or when.

05 As-Is Process Summary

In BPMN terms, the as-is process has two parallel lanes — Customer Service and Operations — that never intersect except in a monthly, manual, meeting-based reconciliation step. That single point of convergence is late, manual, and produces no reusable output: the reconciliation is redone from scratch every month because nothing from it is fed back into either source system.

The core structural problem the future-state design has to solve isn't the reconciliation meeting itself — it's the absence of a shared identifier and a shared data store before that meeting happens.

06 Future State Proposal

Introduce a lightweight Complaint Intake Form that captures route and trip using the same reference data SAP uses (route ID, scheduled trip number, date), instead of free text. Complaint records land in a shared database table alongside a nightly operational performance extract sourced from Mobile Statistics. The datasets are joined using route ID, trip number, and service date, creating a unified reporting layer for operational and customer-service analysis.

A single Power BI dashboard, refreshed daily, replaces the manual monthly summary — visible to Customer Service, Operations, and Duty Managers simultaneously, so reconciliation becomes a by-product of daily reporting rather than a monthly exercise.

Future State Vision

A single reporting platform combines route performance data, ticketing device monitoring, complaint management, SLA compliance tracking, and regulatory performance indicators.

Automated alerts identify:

  • Ticketing devices that have stopped reporting
  • Complaint SLA breaches
  • Deteriorating route performance
  • Recurring breach-code trends
  • Emerging compliance risks

Rather than waiting for month-end KPI reviews, managers gain daily visibility into operational performance, allowing issues to be investigated and resolved earlier.

09 Acceptance Criteria

US-01Structured route/trip selection
GIVEN the officer opens the intake form
WHEN they begin typing a route number
THEN only valid SAP route IDs are offered, and free-text entry is disabled
US-03Side-by-side KPI and complaint view
GIVEN a route and date range are selected on the dashboard
WHEN the page loads
THEN complaint volume and punctuality KPI for that route and range are shown on the same chart, using the same date axis
US-04Conflict flagging
GIVEN a route's complaint volume rises more than 20% week-on-week
AND its punctuality KPI changes by less than 2 percentage points in the same week
THEN the route is flagged on the dashboard's exception list

10 Business Rules

BR-01
A complaint cannot be submitted without a valid route ID and trip number matched against the current SAP reference list.
BR-02
Complaint reason must be selected from the approved category list; "Other" requires a mandatory free-text note capped at 200 characters, reviewed weekly to identify new recurring categories.
BR-03
A complaint may only be edited by the officer who logged it or a Team Lead, and every edit is timestamped and attributed.
BR-04
The punctuality KPI for a route is defined as the percentage of scheduled trips departing within 5 minutes of timetable, consistent with the existing Mobile Statistics reporting methodology — the new process does not redefine the KPI, only reconciles it against complaints.
BR-05
A route is added to the exception list when complaint volume rises more than 20% week-on-week without a corresponding punctuality KPI change of at least 2 percentage points.

12 SQL Analysis Approach

The core analytical task is joining complaint_log to operational_performance_extract on route, trip, and date, then aggregating both to a weekly grain per route so the two measures are comparable on the same axis.

Weekly complaint vs. punctuality by route

-- Weekly complaint count and punctuality rate per route,
-- used to power the dashboard's side-by-side trend view
WITH weekly_complaints AS (
    SELECT
        route_id,
        DATE_TRUNC('week', complaint_date) AS week_start,
        COUNT(*) AS complaint_count
    FROM complaint_log
    GROUP BY route_id, DATE_TRUNC('week', complaint_date)
),
weekly_punctuality AS (
    SELECT
        route_id,
        DATE_TRUNC('week', service_date) AS week_start,
        AVG(CASE WHEN status = 'ON_TIME' THEN 1.0 ELSE 0.0 END) AS on_time_rate
    FROM operational_performance_extract
    GROUP BY route_id, DATE_TRUNC('week', service_date)
)
SELECT
    COALESCE(c.route_id, p.route_id)   AS route_id,
    COALESCE(c.week_start, p.week_start) AS week_start,
    COALESCE(c.complaint_count, 0)     AS complaint_count,
    ROUND(p.on_time_rate * 100, 1)     AS on_time_pct
FROM weekly_complaints c
FULL OUTER JOIN weekly_punctuality p
    ON c.route_id = p.route_id AND c.week_start = p.week_start
ORDER BY route_id, week_start;

Exception flag: complaints rising without a KPI move

A window function compares each route's current week to the prior week, applying business rule BR-05:

-- Flags routes matching the exception rule (BR-05)
WITH weekly AS (
    -- (same aggregation as above, aliased as weekly)
    SELECT route_id, week_start, complaint_count, on_time_pct
    FROM weekly_summary
),
with_prior AS (
    SELECT
        *,
        LAG(complaint_count) OVER (PARTITION BY route_id ORDER BY week_start) AS prior_complaints,
        LAG(on_time_pct)    OVER (PARTITION BY route_id ORDER BY week_start) AS prior_on_time_pct
    FROM weekly
)
SELECT
    route_id,
    week_start,
    complaint_count,
    prior_complaints,
    ROUND(on_time_pct - prior_on_time_pct, 1) AS kpi_point_change
FROM with_prior
WHERE prior_complaints > 0
  AND (complaint_count - prior_complaints) / prior_complaints::FLOAT > 0.20
  AND ABS(on_time_pct - prior_on_time_pct) < 2.0;

This query result feeds the dashboard's exception list directly — the same logic is mirrored as a DAX measure in Power BI so the flag stays consistent whether a user is looking at the pre-built dashboard or an ad-hoc extract.

Key DAX measures

On-Time % = 
DIVIDE(
    CALCULATE(COUNTROWS(operational_performance_extract), operational_performance_extract[status] = "ON_TIME"),
    COUNTROWS(operational_performance_extract)
)

Complaint Growth WoW = 
VAR CurrentWeek = [Complaint Count]
VAR PriorWeek = CALCULATE([Complaint Count], DATEADD('Calendar'[Date], -7, DAY))
RETURN DIVIDE(CurrentWeek - PriorWeek, PriorWeek)

Is Exception Route = 
IF([Complaint Growth WoW] > 0.20 && ABS([On-Time % Change WoW]) < 0.02, "Flagged", "Normal")

Page purpose

  • Overview — for Senior Management: network-level KPIs, refreshed daily, replacing the old monthly manual summary.
  • Route Drill-Down — for Operations and Duty Managers: filter to one route, see complaint reason breakdown against punctuality trend.
  • Exceptions — for Duty Managers: the BR-05 flagged list, sorted by complaint growth, for daily triage rather than monthly discovery.

The primary benefit of the proposed solution is improved visibility across operational performance, regulatory compliance, ticketing device monitoring, and customer service activity. By combining these data sources into a single reporting environment, management can identify emerging issues earlier, prioritise investigations more effectively, and intervene before performance targets or service-level obligations are breached. The solution also supports stronger governance by creating a consistent reporting framework that can be used by Operations, Technology Development, Customer Service, and Senior Management.

15 Lessons Learned

→
The hardest part of this problem was never the SQL or the dashboard — it was recognising that two teams were arguing about numbers because their systems didn't share a key. A BSA's first job is often finding that missing key, not building the report.
→
Designing the intake form to reuse SAP's own reference data (rather than creating a new mapping table) was the single decision that made everything downstream — the joins, the DAX, the exception logic — reliable instead of approximate.
→
Writing the business rules (BR-01 to BR-05) before the SQL forced me to make the exception threshold explicit and defensible, rather than an arbitrary line buried in a query.
→
Drafting acceptance criteria in Given/When/Then made it obvious where a requirement was still ambiguous — "validated list" wasn't testable until I specified what happens when an officer types an invalid route.
