I spent seven years as a Clerical Officer at Dublin Bus, working inside a live, regulated, high-volume operation. That role put me in daily contact with SAP data, KPI reporting, and the manual processes that sit underneath them — the ones that never make it into a slide deck. I saw, repeatedly, where a process broke down before the numbers ever showed it.

Rather than wait for a KPI to flag a problem, I started mapping the processes myself, asking stakeholders what they actually needed, and writing the queries to check whether the data agreed. That's the job of a Business Systems Analyst — I was already doing pieces of it without the title or the toolset.

This portfolio is me formalising that: five projects that take a real operational problem through the full BSA lifecycle — process mapping, requirements gathering, SQL analysis, and Power BI reporting — the same way I'd approach a problem on day one of a BSA role.
